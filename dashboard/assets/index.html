Lantern works by connecting users around the world together in a peer-to-peer network. By signing in with your Google account, you allow Lantern to take advantage of any trusted relationships between you and any contacts you may have on the other side of the censorship divide. Knowing about trusted relationships is critical to the speed and safety of Lanternâ€™s design.

Those using Lantern to get access choose which of their Google contacts they would trust to proxy any unencrypted traffic. Whenever these trusted contacts are connected to Lantern, Lantern can securely route traffic through them from the people who trust them, even when the destination site does not offer an encrypted (https) connection. If everyone on the Lantern network were connected anonymously, Lantern would not be able to take advantage of these trusted relationships, and the network would be much less efficient.

When you visit a web page whose url begins with http://, content is transferred between your computer and the computer hosting the web site in plaintext. This means that there is nothing stopping any intermediary along the trafficâ€™s path (such as your internet service provider, the internet service provider of the web site, or anyone in between) from reading the traffic.

Ideally, all web sites would transfer content only over https, a secure version of http which encrypts traffic to prevent intermediaries from being able to read it. Some web sites offer both http and https, but unfortunately, many web sites transfer content over http only.

When Lantern reroutes your traffic through another userâ€™s connection, it checks to see whether the web site youâ€™re visiting offers https. If so, Lantern can safely route your traffic through any other Lantern user without fear that they could read your traffic.

However, if the web site offers only http, Lantern takes extra measures to protect your traffic. First, Lantern adds its own layer of encryption between you and the Lantern peer proxying your traffic. This means that your traffic to sites that offer only http is still encrypted before it leaves or arrives at your computer, which prevents your internet service provider from being able to read it. When it reaches your Lantern peer, however, it must be decrypted before it can proceed, since the destination web site is http-only. It is therefore necessary that only peers whose connections you trust be used to proxy traffic to http-only web sites.

Lantern Cloud Proxies are used when you donâ€™t have any trusted peers online whose uncensored connections can be used to proxy your traffic. In that case, Lantern Cloud Proxies act as a backup, available to proxy your traffic 24/7.

Lantern takes the responsibility of proxying your traffic very seriously. We have an explicit interest in keeping any information about proxied traffic secret and ephemeral, i.e. not stored, analyzed, or shared with anyone. Any human contact with traffic information is limited to only the core Lantern developers and is strictly incidental to making sure Lantern works properly.
